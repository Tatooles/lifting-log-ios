Privacy Policy

How Lifting Log handles your data

Effective date: June 27, 2026. This policy explains workout data, account data, sync, export, and deletion.

What you should know in 30 seconds

  • You can use Lifting Log locally without creating an account.
  • Signing in enables sync through Clerk authentication and Convex account-backed storage.
  • You can export workout history and delete account-backed or local data from Settings.
  • No analytics. No ads. No tracking.

Data Stored on Device

Lifting Log stores workout-related data such as exercises, sets, workout sessions, notes, settings, and workout history. You can use the app locally on your iPhone without creating an account.

Authentication

If you create an account, Lifting Log uses Clerk for authentication. Clerk may process account information such as your email address, authentication identifiers, session information, and Sign in with Apple details.

If you use Sign in with Apple and choose Hide My Email, Apple may provide a private relay email address instead of your personal email address.

Sync

When you sign in, Lifting Log uses Convex to sync supported workout, exercise, settings, and history data for your account. Sync data is scoped to your authenticated account.

Exporting & Deleting Data

  • You can export workout history from Settings.
  • Signed-in users can delete their account and account-backed data from Settings under Privacy & Data.
  • Signed-out users can delete local data stored on the device from Settings under Privacy & Data.

No Analytics, Ads, or Tracking

No analytics. No ads. No tracking.

Lifting Log does not use third-party advertising, cross-app tracking, or product analytics in the v1 release. Standard platform and provider diagnostics may be processed by Apple, Clerk, Convex, or hosting providers to keep their services reliable and secure.

Support

For privacy questions or support, email support@liftinglog.app.
